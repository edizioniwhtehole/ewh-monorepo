## 8. Key Risks (And How to Mitigate)

### Risk 1: Regulatory (Fund Management Rules)

**Risk**: Taking equity = acting as VC = need license in most countries

**Regulations**:
- **EU**: AIFMD (Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive)
  - Need license if >‚Ç¨100M AUM or >500 investors
  - Exemption: Small funds (<‚Ç¨100M, <150 investors)

- **Italy**: Need authorization from CONSOB if >‚Ç¨500M AUM

- **US**: Need SEC registration if fund >$150M or >100 investors

**Mitigation**:
- ‚úÖ Start small (<‚Ç¨100M portfolio, <150 investors)
- ‚úÖ Each deal is bilateral (not a "fund" legally, just individual agreements)
- ‚úÖ Hire regulatory lawyer (‚Ç¨20k) to structure correctly
- ‚úÖ Register as fund manager ONLY when you exceed thresholds

**Precedent**: AngelList operated for years before registering as fund manager

### Risk 2: Valuation Complexity (How to Value Equity?)

**Risk**: Equity is illiquid, how do you value it for financial statements?

**Accounting**:
- IAS 39 / IFRS 9: Equity investments at fair value
- Mark-to-market quarterly
- If no recent funding: Use valuation model (DCF, comparables)

**For Financial Statements**:
- Conservative: Value at cost (‚Ç¨1.2k per startup)
- Moderate: Value at last funding round valuation √ó your %
- Aggressive: Mark-to-market using comparable public companies

**Mitigation**:
- ‚úÖ Hire accounting firm familiar with VC accounting
- ‚úÖ Get quarterly 409A valuations for portfolio (standard for VCs)
- ‚úÖ Disclose methodology clearly to investors

### Risk 3: Misalignment (Startups Give Equity But Don't Use Software)

**Risk**: Startup takes deal, uses software for 1 month, then switches to competitor

**Contract Clauses**:
- ‚úÖ Equity is perpetual (keep it even if they stop using)
- ‚úÖ But: Support + updates conditional on usage
- ‚úÖ Clawback: If they stop using within 12 months, equity reverts

**Incentives**:
- ‚úÖ Make software so good they don't want to switch
- ‚úÖ Integration lock-in (switching cost is high)
- ‚úÖ Community lock-in (network effects with other portfolio companies)

### Risk 4: Adverse Selection 2.0 (Bad Startups Take Deal, Good Ones Don't)

**Risk**: YC/Sequoia-backed startups say no, only desperate ones say yes

**Mitigation**:
- ‚úÖ Be VERY selective (reject 70-80%)
- ‚úÖ Showcase top startups who took deal (social proof)
- ‚úÖ Partner with top VCs ("Recommended for YC/Techstars portfolio")
- ‚úÖ Make it prestigious (acceptance = validation)

**Benchmark**: YC accepts 1-2% ‚Üí creates scarcity ‚Üí everyone wants in

### Risk 5: Conflict of Interest (You See Competitors' Data)

**Risk**: Real estate startup A and B both use your platform, you see both CRMs

**Legal**:
- ‚úÖ Strict confidentiality in contract
- ‚úÖ Chinese wall (different team members for competing companies)
- ‚úÖ Anonymized analytics (can't see Company A's specific data)

**Ethical**:
- ‚úÖ Don't share data between competitors
- ‚úÖ Don't invest in one and sabotage another
- ‚úÖ Reputation is everything (lose trust = lose business)
